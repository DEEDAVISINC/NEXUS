1. Look up the bid on BidNet/MITN
2. See the specifications
3. Contact Canton Township directly

- They lose incentive to give you their best price
- You lose your competitive advantage

## üìã **PROCESS FOR QUOTE REQUESTS**

### **Step 1: Extract Specifications**
- Read the bid/RFP
- **Write down ONLY the product specs** (items, quantities, part numbers)
- Create a clean list with NO identifying information

### **Step 2: Prepare Your Request**
- Use generic terminology ("municipal client", "Michigan project")
- Type out specs manually (don't attach bid PDFs)
- Use general delivery location (county/region, not specific address)

### **Step 3: Contact Suppliers**
- Phone or email with ONLY product specifications
- Request competitive pricing
- Don't mention government/municipal pricing tiers
- Get NET 30 or better terms

### **Step 4: Receive Quotes**
- Collect supplier pricing
- Add your margin (15-30% depending on complexity)
- Build your bid response

### **Step 5: Submit Bid**
- Submit to government agency directly
- Use their portal/process
- Include all required forms and certifications

### **Step 6: If You Win**
- Purchase from supplier at quoted price
- Coordinate delivery (you remain the point of contact)
- Invoice government agency
- Collect payment
- Pay supplier

## üö® **RED FLAGS - WHEN TO BE EXTRA CAREFUL**

### **High-Risk Situations:**

**1. Local Suppliers:**
- If you use a supplier in the same city as the government agency
- They likely already know the procurement officers
- Be even more vague about client identity

**2. Specialty Items:**
- Unique products with limited suppliers
- They might guess the project from specifications
- Emphasize you're the contracting party

**3. Large Dollar Amounts:**
- High-value contracts are attractive to suppliers
- They have more incentive to try direct bidding
- Be extremely protective of client identity

**4. Suppliers Who Push Back:**
- If they insist on knowing the end buyer
- If they ask for delivery addresses before quoting
- If they want to "verify the opportunity"
- **Consider using a different supplier!**
