## üí° PRO TIPS

### **Negotiation Tips:**
1. **Get multiple quotes** - Use them to negotiate lower rates
2. **Offer volume** - "This is 40-60 properties, steady work for 6 months"
3. **Mention reliability** - "Government contract, reliable payment"
4. **Build relationship** - "Could lead to more contracts in other cities"
5. **Ask for best price** - "What's your best rate for contractor work?"

### **Risk Management:**
1. **Always have 2 providers** - One primary, one backup
2. **Get insurance certificates NOW** - Before bidding
3. **Put terms in writing** - Simple agreement on rates/payment
4. **Test them first** - If possible, hire them for a small test job
5. **Build in buffer** - Don't use razor-thin margins

### **If Quotes Are Too High (Over $35/cut):**
- Ask: "What would make it worth $30/cut? More volume? Longer commitment?"
- Consider: Using 2-3 smaller providers rotating
- Option: Take smaller margin (bid $40-42 instead of $38)
- Last resort: Skip this bid, wait for better opportunity
